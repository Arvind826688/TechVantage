Data Protection Policy

Our commitment to protecting your personal data and privacy

Last Updated: December 2024

1. Introduction

TechVantage is committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal data. This Data Protection Policy outlines our approach to data protection, compliance with applicable laws including the Personal Data Protection Bill (India), and our commitment to maintaining the highest standards of data security.

Security First: Data protection is not just a legal requirement for usâ€”it's a fundamental principle that guides every aspect of our operations and service delivery.

2. Legal Framework and Compliance

2.1 Applicable Laws

Our data protection practices comply with:

  • Personal Data Protection Bill, India (when enacted)
  • Information Technology Act, 2000 and Rules
  • Indian Contract Act, 1872
  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019
  • Relevant state and local regulations in Rajasthan

2.2 International Standards

We also align our practices with internationally recognized standards:

  • ISO 27001 Information Security Management
  • GDPR principles (for EU clients)
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework
  • SOC 2 Type II compliance principles

3. Types of Data We Process

Personal Data

  • Name and contact information
  • Business and professional details
  • Communication preferences
  • Account credentials

Business Data

  • Company information
  • Financial and billing data
  • Project requirements
  • Service usage analytics

Technical Data

  • System logs and metrics
  • API usage data
  • Performance analytics
  • Error and debugging information

AI Training Data

  • Anonymized interaction patterns
  • Aggregated usage statistics
  • Model performance metrics
  • Quality improvement feedback

4. Data Collection Principles

4.1 Lawful Basis for Processing

We process personal data based on:

  • Consent: Explicit consent for marketing and optional services
  • Contract Performance: Processing necessary to deliver our services
  • Legal Obligation: Compliance with applicable laws and regulations
  • Legitimate Interest: Business operations and service improvement

4.2 Data Minimization

Principle: We collect only the personal data that is necessary for specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes. We do not collect excessive or irrelevant information.

4.3 Purpose Limitation

Personal data is collected for specific purposes and not used in ways incompatible with those purposes:

  • Service delivery and project management
  • Customer support and communication
  • Billing and financial management
  • Legal compliance and record keeping
  • Service improvement (with appropriate safeguards)

5. Data Processing Activities

5.1 Service Delivery Processing

Our Data Processing Workflow:

1
Collection: Gather necessary data through forms, consultations, and service interactions
2
Processing: Use data to deliver services, create solutions, and manage projects
3
Storage: Securely store data in encrypted, access-controlled systems
4
Retention: Keep data only as long as necessary for service delivery and legal compliance
5
Disposal: Securely delete data when no longer needed

5.2 AI and Machine Learning Processing

Special considerations for AI-related data processing:

  • Anonymization: Personal identifiers removed before AI training
  • Aggregation: Data combined with other sources to prevent identification
  • Purpose Binding: AI training data used only for service improvement
  • Algorithmic Accountability: Regular audits of AI decision-making processes

6. Your Data Protection Rights

Right to Access

Request a copy of personal data we hold about you, including how it's used and who it's shared with.

Right to Rectification

Correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

Right to Erasure

Request deletion of your personal data when it's no longer necessary or you withdraw consent.

Right to Restrict Processing

Limit how we process your personal data in certain circumstances.

Right to Data Portability

Receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.

Right to Object

Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

6.1 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights:

  1. Submit a written request to dpo@techvantage.in
  2. Provide sufficient information to verify your identity
  3. Specify which right you wish to exercise
  4. We will respond within 30 days of receiving your request

7. Data Security Measures

7.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption for data at rest and TLS 1.3 for data in transit
  • Access Controls: Role-based access with multi-factor authentication
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection, and VPN access
  • Backup Security: Encrypted backups with tested recovery procedures
  • Endpoint Protection: Advanced threat protection on all devices

7.2 Organizational Safeguards

  • Staff Training: Regular data protection and security awareness training
  • Access Management: Principle of least privilege for data access
  • Incident Response: Comprehensive data breach response procedures
  • Vendor Management: Due diligence and contracts for third-party processors
  • Regular Audits: Internal and external security assessments

7.3 AI-Specific Security

  • Model Security: Protection against adversarial attacks and data poisoning
  • Training Data Security: Secure handling of data used for AI model training
  • Output Monitoring: Continuous monitoring of AI outputs for privacy leaks
  • Explainability: Maintaining transparency in AI decision-making processes

8. Data Transfers and Sharing

8.1 Domestic Transfers

Data may be processed in different locations within India:

  • Our primary data center in Mumbai
  • Backup facilities in Bangalore
  • Cloud infrastructure within Indian borders
  • Partner facilities (with appropriate safeguards)

8.2 International Transfers

If international data transfer is necessary:

  • We obtain explicit consent or ensure legal basis exists
  • Implement appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses)
  • Ensure the receiving country has adequate protection levels
  • Maintain records of all international transfers

8.3 Third-Party Sharing

We only share data with trusted partners who:

  • Have signed comprehensive data processing agreements
  • Implement equivalent security measures
  • Process data only for specified purposes
  • Submit to regular security audits

9. Data Retention and Disposal

9.1 Retention Periods

  • Project Data: 3 years after project completion
  • Financial Records: 7 years as per Indian tax laws
  • Communication Records: 2 years for support purposes
  • Marketing Data: Until consent is withdrawn or 5 years maximum
  • Legal/Compliance Data: As required by applicable laws

9.2 Secure Disposal

When data reaches end of retention period:

  • Data is permanently deleted using DoD 5220.22-M standards
  • Physical media is destroyed or professionally wiped
  • Disposal activities are logged and audited
  • Certificates of destruction are maintained

10. Data Breach Management

10.1 Breach Response Process

  1. Detection and Assessment (0-1 hours):
    • Immediate containment of the breach
    • Assessment of scope and impact
    • Activation of incident response team
  2. Investigation and Documentation (1-24 hours):
    • Detailed forensic investigation
    • Documentation of affected data and individuals
    • Root cause analysis
  3. Notification (24-72 hours):
    • Notification to affected individuals
    • Reporting to relevant authorities
    • Communication to stakeholders
  4. Remediation and Recovery (Ongoing):
    • Implementation of corrective measures
    • Support for affected individuals
    • System hardening and process improvements

11. Accountability and Governance

11.1 Data Protection Officer (DPO)

Our DPO is responsible for:

  • Monitoring compliance with data protection laws
  • Conducting privacy impact assessments
  • Serving as point of contact for data subjects and authorities
  • Providing data protection training and guidance

11.2 Privacy by Design

We implement privacy considerations:

  • At the design stage of new systems and processes
  • Through regular privacy impact assessments
  • By default settings that protect privacy
  • Throughout the entire data lifecycle

11.3 Regular Reviews and Updates

  • Annual review of this Data Protection Policy
  • Quarterly assessment of data processing activities
  • Continuous monitoring of legal and regulatory changes
  • Regular training and awareness programs for staff

12. Complaints and Enforcement

12.1 Internal Complaints Process

  1. Submit complaint to dpo@techvantage.in
  2. Acknowledgment within 2 business days
  3. Investigation completed within 30 days
  4. Written response with resolution or explanation

12.2 External Complaint Options

If unsatisfied with our response, you may contact:

  • National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission
  • Cyber Crime Cell, Jaipur Police
  • Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In)
  • Relevant sectoral regulators
